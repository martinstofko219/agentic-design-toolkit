# design-grill

Pressure-test a design idea before any design work begins — so you're solving the right problem, not just the obvious one.

---

## Overview

It's easy to jump into wireframes or prototypes with a concept that feels solid but hasn't been properly interrogated. This skill slows that down in a useful way. It interviews you about your idea in logical phases — batching related questions together — probing the problem definition, the users, the core flows, the edge cases, and the constraints you might not have thought through yet. The point isn't to block progress; it's to make sure the work that follows is built on something real.

---

## When to use it

Use this skill before committing to a design direction, especially when:

- The problem statement is assumed rather than validated
- You haven't thought through failure states or edge cases
- You want to stress-test an idea before presenting it
- You need a written summary of the concept to hand off or carry into wireframing

Good triggers include: "grill me on this," "poke holes in this," "stress-test this idea," "challenge my thinking," or "help me think through this before I start."

---

## How to invoke

**In an agent session (Copilot or Claude):** Describe your design idea — even if it's half-formed — and the agent will invoke the skill. It'll start asking questions immediately.

**Manually:** Add `SKILL.md` as context and share your concept. The more honest you are about what you don't know yet, the more useful the interrogation will be.

---

## What you get

By the end of the session, you'll have a written summary covering: the problem as it's now understood, who the primary users are and what context they're operating in, the core interaction flows, the key risks and open questions that remain, and a clear statement of what you're ready to do next.

That summary is the deliverable. It's not a design artifact — it's a foundation document you can hand off, use to kick off wireframing, or share with stakeholders to align on scope before anything gets built.

---

## Good to know

The skill grills in phases — each phase batches as many related questions as it needs, and every question comes with a recommended answer, so you're reacting and refining rather than answering from scratch. The number of phases adapts to how well-formed the concept is: vague ideas get more, smaller rounds; developed ones get fewer, broader rounds. Expect the conversation to take a handful of exchanges rather than a long back-and-forth.

This skill is diagnostic, not generative. It won't produce screens, specs, or prototypes. What it produces is clarity — and a document that makes the next step significantly faster.
